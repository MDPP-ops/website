<!-- WORK PACKAGES -->
<section class="py-5 bg-light border-top">
  <div class="container">
    <div class="row g-4">

      <div class="col-md-6">
        <div class="soft-card p-4 h-100">
          <div class="d-flex align-items-start gap-3 mb-3">
            <div class="icon-badge">1</div>
            <div>
              <div class="fw-semibold">Requirements and stakeholder alignment</div>
            </div>
          </div>
          <p class="text-secondary mb-0">
            Map the regulatory, technical, and organisational requirements for molecular-level
            DPPs in the textile sector. This work package brings together SMEs, recyclers,
            policymakers, and standards bodies to establish shared definitions, use cases,
            and success criteria that guide the rest of the project.
          </p>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="col-md-6">
        <div class="soft-card p-4 h-100">
          <div class="d-flex align-items-start gap-3 mb-3">
            <div class="icon-badge">2</div>
            <div>
              <div class="fw-semibold">Data model and identifiers</div>
            </div>
          </div>
          <p class="text-secondary mb-0">
            Develop a data model that links material identity at the molecular level to components
            and finished products. This includes defining how molecular fingerprints, composition
            data, and provenance information are structured, stored, and referenced within a
            DPP-compatible framework.
          </p>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="col-md-6">
        <div class="soft-card p-4 h-100">
          <div class="d-flex align-items-start gap-3 mb-3">
            <div class="icon-badge">3</div>
            <div>
              <div class="fw-semibold">Technical architecture</div>
            </div>
          </div>
          <p class="text-secondary mb-0">
            Design and prototype the infrastructure for capturing molecular data, storing it
            in an open reference database, and exchanging it across the value chain. The
            architecture is built on open standards and accessible APIs, with the aim of
            interoperating with emerging European data spaces and reporting systems (e.g., EPR).
          </p>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="col-md-6">
        <div class="soft-card p-4 h-100">
          <div class="d-flex align-items-start gap-3 mb-3">
            <div class="icon-badge">4</div>
            <div>
              <div class="fw-semibold">Pilots and evaluation</div>
            </div>
          </div>
          <p class="text-secondary mb-0">
            Test the developed methods and infrastructure in real-world settings with Dutch
            textile partners. Industry Living Labs with byBorre, Knitwear Lab, and New Order
            of Fashion (NOoF) evaluate what works, what breaks, and what scales. A citizen-science
            component investigates how end-users interact with DPP information, including aspects
            of digital literacy, trust, and behaviour.
          </p>
        </div>
      </div>

    </div>
  </div>
</section>
