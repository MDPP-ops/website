<!-- WHY (RESEARCH GAP) -->
<section id="why" class="py-5 bg-light border-top">
  <div class="container">

    <div class="row mb-4">
      <div class="col-lg-9">
        <h2 class="fw-bold">The research challenge</h2>
        <p class="text-secondary mb-0">
          The EU is mandating Digital Product Passports under ESPR, yet the predominantly SME-based textile
          industry lacks practical methods for persistent material verification. Conventional identifiers&mdash;QR
          codes, RFID tags, printed labels&mdash;degrade, detach, or get lost during use, creating a traceability
          gap exactly when verification is needed most. Existing DPP approaches do not address how to tie
          passport data back to the physical material itself.
        </p>
      </div>
    </div>

    <div class="row g-3">
      <div class="col-md-6 col-lg-4">
        <div class="bg-white border rounded-4 p-4 h-100">
          <div class="fw-semibold">Identity beyond labels</div>
          <p class="text-secondary mb-0">
            M-DPP investigates whether molecular fingerprints embedded in the textile material
            itself can serve as a durable, tamper-resistant alternative to external identifiers.
          </p>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="col-md-6 col-lg-4">
        <div class="bg-white border rounded-4 p-4 h-100">
          <div class="fw-semibold">Verifiable claims</div>
          <p class="text-secondary mb-0">
            The project studies how composition, recycled content, and processing history can be
            independently verified using established analytical methods (FTIR, Raman, chromatography).
          </p>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="col-md-6 col-lg-4">
        <div class="bg-white border rounded-4 p-4 h-100">
          <div class="fw-semibold">Open and interoperable by design</div>
          <p class="text-secondary mb-0">
            Rather than proposing a proprietary platform, M-DPP explores open standards and
            modular architectures that allow different actors to participate on equal terms.
          </p>
        </div>
      </div>
    </div>

  </div>
</section>

<!-- RESEARCH APPROACH -->
<section id="how" class="py-5">
  <div class="container">

    <div class="row mb-4">
      <div class="col-lg-9">
        <div class="section-kicker mb-2">Research approach</div>
        <h2 class="fw-bold">From molecular analysis to interoperable data exchange.</h2>
        <p class="text-secondary mb-0">
          The project follows a design-science methodology, combining material science with information
          systems research. Knowledge is generated by building, testing, and evaluating working prototypes
          in realistic contexts with real companies.
        </p>
      </div>
    </div>

    <div class="row g-3">
      <div class="col-md-6 col-lg-3">
        <div class="border rounded-4 p-4 h-100">
          <div class="fw-semibold">1. Characterise</div>
          <div class="text-secondary">
            Develop and refine molecular fingerprinting methods (FTIR, Raman spectroscopy,
            chromatography, model-assisted image analysis) to identify fibre composition in
            textiles&mdash;including blends, recycled content, and aged materials.
          </div>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="col-md-6 col-lg-3">
        <div class="border rounded-4 p-4 h-100">
          <div class="fw-semibold">2. Validate</div>
          <div class="text-secondary">
            Test whether molecular signatures remain reliable after real-world use:
            washing, wearing, ageing, and processing. Assess the limits and confidence
            levels of each analytical method.
          </div>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="col-md-6 col-lg-3">
        <div class="border rounded-4 p-4 h-100">
          <div class="fw-semibold">3. Publish</div>
          <div class="text-secondary">
            Build an open-source reference database of textile molecular profiles,
            enabling independent validation of composition and sustainability claims
            across the sector.
          </div>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="col-md-6 col-lg-3">
        <div class="border rounded-4 p-4 h-100">
          <div class="fw-semibold">4. Integrate</div>
          <div class="text-secondary">
            Design and prototype a modular, ESPR-aligned DPP architecture using open standards
            and accessible APIs&mdash;exploring how it can connect to European data spaces
            and support reporting obligations (e.g., EPR).
          </div>
        </div>
      </div>
    </div>

  </div>
</section>
